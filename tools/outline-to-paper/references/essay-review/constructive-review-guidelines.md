# Constructive Review Guidelines

Use these characteristics for every review you write.

## Characteristics of Constructive Reviews

### Specific

- Be concrete.
- Prefer precise observations to vague reactions.
- Example: "the font in Figure 2 is a bit small"

### Gives examples

- Ground criticism in examples when possible.
- Example: "Sometimes the coverage type is mandated, for example MCDC in
  aerospace."

### Relevant

- Focus on the most pertinent aspects of the work.
- Prioritize issues that affect the core claims, evidence, and clarity.

### Nuanced

- Distinguish strong parts from weak parts.
- Example: "Section 1 was clear, but I found Section 2 hard to follow."

### Respectful

- Treat the authors as capable of making the necessary improvements.
- Avoid dismissive or hostile language.

### Humble

- Write from your perspective.
- Example: "I prefer", not "everyone prefers".
